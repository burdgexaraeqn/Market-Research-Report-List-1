<p><p>The diabetes treatment market comprises three primary types: insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, and non-insulin injectable drugs. Insulin is crucial for blood sugar regulation, particularly in Type 1 diabetes and advanced Type 2 diabetes. Oral hypoglycemic agents work by enhancing insulin sensitivity or reducing glucose production, suitable for many Type 2 diabetes patients. Non-insulin injectable drugs, including GLP-1 receptor agonists, help lower blood sugar by mimicking natural hormones. Each type plays a vital role in managing diabetes effectively.</p></p>
